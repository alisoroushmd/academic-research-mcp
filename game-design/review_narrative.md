# Narrative Review: Crescent Imperium v1

**Reviewer frame:** theme-mechanic coherence. Does the *feel* of an action match the *weight* of what the player just did in fiction?

## 1. Legitimacy: currency, or lived pressure?

Right now Legitimacy reads as a second mana pool with moral flavor text. You spend it, you regain it, it gates conversion. What it *doesn't* do is force you to humiliate yourself to keep it — which is what John Company's lobbying does so well. In the real region, legitimacy is purchased through costly, specific performances: a pilgrimage funded, a martyr eulogized, a US president hosted, a cleric appeased. Proposal: **Legitimacy cannot be spent as a number. It must be spent as a Patron token placed on a specific domestic constituency card (Clergy, Officer Corps, Bazaar, Diaspora, Street).** Each constituency demands upkeep actions every Era, and failing one doesn't just cost points — it *locks* an action type (you can't mobilize without the Officer Corps; you can't borrow without the Bazaar). That transforms Legitimacy from a bar into a roomful of people you must face.

## 2. Proxies: the off-script moment

A hidden token captures sponsorship but not the thing that actually haunts proxy relationships: the proxy having its own project. Exposure is only one failure mode; the more thematic one is *autonomy*. Proposal: **every Proxy Cell has a face-down Agenda card drawn from a mixed deck (sponsor's goals + the proxy's own goals). Once per Era, during the Crisis Phase, one random active Proxy "acts on its Agenda" whether or not the sponsor wanted it to.** If it's the sponsor's agenda, free action. If it's the proxy's — a bombing, a political assassination, a unilateral ceasefire — the sponsor must either disavow (lose the asset + Legitimacy for abandonment) or endorse (absorb the consequences). That is the Hezbollah/IRGC, CIA/Contra, ISI/Taliban relationship in miniature.

## 3. Era transitions: flips need fulcrums

"Your board flips" is administratively elegant and thematically mute. You need a moment the table remembers. Do it without railroading by making the *trigger* player-generated: each Era has 2–3 **Fulcrum Conditions** on hidden cards (e.g., "a monarchy loses its capital region," "nuclear doctrine crosses threshold," "two Great Powers vote opposite at Summit"). Whoever's action trips a Fulcrum narrates it, names it, and chooses between two listed consequences. The 1979, 1991, 2001, 2011 moments then feel *caused* rather than scheduled. This is also how you honor Goal #2 (pressures, not scripts) mechanically rather than rhetorically.

## 4. Oil: the curse dimension

Currently oil is flavor-wrapped money. The curse is missing entirely. Proposal: **every Oil-producing Theater accumulates a Rentier token each time it pays out above a threshold. Rentier tokens are permanent; they reduce that Theater's contribution to the owner's Legitimacy recovery and increase the cost of domestic Doctrine purchases.** Now the windfall feels like a windfall *and* a slow poisoning. Selling down production to de-rentier becomes a real, painful strategic choice — the Gulf's actual dilemma.

## 5. Civilians: the street as weather

Don't make them a seventh faction — make them a **pressure system with memory**. Each Theater tracks a Street value; violence, sanctions, and refugees raise it. When Street crosses thresholds, any player may spend Intel to "read the square" and trigger events drawn from a Street deck (Protest, Strike, Exodus, Uprising) targeting the *currently legitimate ruler* of that Theater. Diaspora becomes a transferable Street token placed off-map that boosts The Movement or a sympathetic faction's Legitimacy. The street is not an opponent; it is what every opponent must account for.

## 6. End-game: bang, not whimper

Status Phase scoring will fizzle. Replace Era 5's final round with a **Final Summit**: every living faction brings its strongest grievance and strongest offer, and resolutions taken here score double but require supermajorities weighted by Legitimacy. A faction locked out of the Summit (too low Legitimacy, sanctioned, at war) gets one **Spoiler Action** — a last-chance war, a leak, a terror spectacular — that can invalidate specific resolutions. The table ends negotiating over the region's future with a gun on the table.

## 7. Table story

With v1 as written, the story is "I beat Dan's Iran." With the additions above, the story becomes "we saw the Shah fall and couldn't stop what came after." That is the difference between a 4X and a historical tragedy.

## Single most important recommendation

**Make Legitimacy a set of constituency relationships, not a number.** Everything else in the game — proxies, oil curse, the street, the Final Summit — gets its thematic teeth from there. Without it, Crescent Imperium is a well-dressed resource game. With it, every action has a face attached to it, and that is what the subject matter demands.
