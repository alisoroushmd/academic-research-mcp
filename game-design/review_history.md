# Review: Crescent Imperium v1 — Historical Plausibility and Structural Accuracy

Reviewer: regional specialist, modern Middle East (1970–present).
Scope: whether the *system* the game models matches how the region actually worked. Not about which events appear on which card.

## 1. Faction carve-up

The 7-slot grid has real defensibility as a playable abstraction, but it flattens structural features that drove the period.

**Egypt absent as a full faction is the single largest error.** Egypt is the demographic anchor of the Arab world (Camp David 1978, Sadat's assassination 1981, Mubarak's 30-year securitized peace, Tahrir 2011, Sisi's 2013 counter-revolution, the IMF-and-Gulf-dependent economy, Suez/Bab el-Mandeb chokepoints, and Al-Azhar as the doctrinal counterweight to Saudi Wahhabism and Iranian Shi'ism). Folding Egypt into "Nile" terrain or into Movement-adjacent flavor misses the pivot state that made or broke every pan-Arab project from Nasserism through the Arab League's post-2011 paralysis. It should be its own faction — the *Praetorian Republic* — with a unique "army-as-economy" mechanic (officer-owned firms, Suez revenue, remittance receipts from the Gulf).

**"The Kingdom" as a single Gulf bloc is a serious reduction.** Saudi Arabia, the UAE, and Qatar have been on opposite sides repeatedly: the 2013 MB crackdown, the 2017–2021 Qatar blockade, Libya 2011 onwards, Sudan 2023. MBZ's UAE is not a Saudi satellite — it is arguably the more consequential actor in Yemen, the Horn, and Israel-normalization (Abraham Accords 2020). Qatar's Al Jazeera and its MB/Hamas sponsorship are a *distinct* ideological current. Minimum fix: split into **Riyadh**, **Abu Dhabi**, **Doha** as sub-factions or as mandatory internal-coalition mechanics inside one seat.

**Kurdish actors as terrain is indefensible over a 50-year arc.** The KDP–PUK split, Halabja 1988, the 1991 no-fly zone, KRG autonomy post-2003, the 2017 independence referendum, and — critically — Rojava / SDF as the US ground partner against ISIS 2014–2019 are not terrain effects. They are agents. At minimum, Kurdistan should be a *non-state player-lite* slot (like Movement) with its own legitimacy track and a structural weakness of being leverageable by four different sponsors simultaneously.

**Afghanistan as "frontier"** works for 1979–89 and arguably 2001–21, but the Soviet withdrawal, Taliban 1.0, 9/11, ISAF, and the 2021 collapse each reshaped Pakistan, Iran, and Gulf jihadist recruitment. It should at least be a Theater with scripted mechanical spillover into Iran (refugees, opium, Hazara) and the Gulf (Deobandi finance).

## 2. The "Movement" identity-swap

Defensible as a play-chair, indefensible as history. PLO (secular-nationalist, Soviet/Arab-state sponsored, territorial claim on a specific piece of land), Hezbollah (Lebanese Shi'a, Iranian-IRGC sponsored, a *state-within-a-state* with a parliamentary bloc and a conventional rocket army), AQ (Sunni transnational-jihadist, anti-regime, de-territorialized), and ISIS (Sunni Salafi-jihadist, *pro*-territorial caliphate, anti-AQ) are not successive generations of the same thing. Collapsing them implies a progression — nationalism → Shi'a resistance → transnational jihad → caliphate — that is narratively tidy and historically false. Hezbollah and AQ were contemporaneous and hostile. ISIS split *from* AQ in 2013–14.

**Alternative:** two or three non-state seats with different logics — a **Resistance** seat (PLO/Hezbollah/Houthi: state-sponsored, territorial, bargains with states), a **Jihad** seat (AQ/ISIS/JN: anti-state, transnational), and optionally a **Brotherhood** seat (MB/Ennahda/Hamas-political-wing: electoral-Islamist, the 2011–13 story). Each has its own legitimacy logic. Sponsors (Iran for Resistance, Qatar/Turkey for Brotherhood, private Gulf donors for Jihad) create asymmetric patronage mechanics.

## 3. Era cuts

The decade-bucketed cuts (1976–85, 1986–95, 1996–2005, 2006–15, 2016–26) miss every actual regime shift. The breakpoints that reorganized the system were **1979** (Iranian revolution, Camp David, Grand Mosque seizure, Soviet invasion of Afghanistan — all in one year), **1990–91** (Kuwait, USSR collapse, Madrid), **2003** (Iraq invasion), **2011** (Arab uprisings), and **2014–15** (ISIS caliphate, JCPOA, Russian Syria intervention, Saudi Yemen war). Recommend: **1976–78 / 1979–90 / 1991–2002 / 2003–10 / 2011–26**, or compress to three Eras (Cold War Arena / Unipolar Interlude / Fragmented Multipolar) as the design doc already flags.

## 4. Oil-as-weather

The shared-weather model captures OPEC bargaining and the producer/importer asymmetry well. It misses three structural shifts:

- **US shale post-2010** turned the Hegemon from price-taker to swing producer and directly enabled the 2014 Saudi price-war, the US's reduced tolerance for Gulf security costs, and the Biden-era fist-bump dynamic. The Hegemon needs a "domestic production" dial that *competes* with Saudi spare capacity.
- **Non-OPEC producers** (Russia as OPEC+ partner from 2016, Azerbaijan, Kazakhstan) and Iranian sanctioned-barrel discounting to China are missing.
- **Gas/LNG** is a separate commodity with separate politics: Qatar's North Field, Iran's South Pars (same field, different politics), the Nord Stream era, and the post-2022 European scramble for Qatari LNG. Treat gas as a *second* track with different geography and different customers (Europe, East Asia).

## 5. Hegemon mechanics

One slot cannot carry US–USSR bipolar, US-unipolar, and post-2015 multipolar. The Soviet/Russian arc in particular is a distinct actor: Afghanistan 1979–89, Tartus basing continuity, the Syria intervention September 2015, Wagner in Libya and Sudan, the S-400 sale to Turkey, the Iran drone-purchase reversal 2022. Russia post-2015 is not an "influence card" — it is a veto-wielding expeditionary power in Syria with a permanent Mediterranean footprint. China is also qualitatively different: not expeditionary, but structurally dominant as the buyer of Iranian and Gulf oil, the Belt-and-Road financier, and the 2023 Saudi-Iran reconciliation broker.

**Fix:** split Hegemon into **Atlantic** (US + UK/France residuals) and **Eurasian** (USSR → Russia + China as a coalition seat or rotating occupant) from 1991 onward, or make the Hegemon a 2-seat structure post-Era 2.

## 6. Structural omissions

- **Migration and remittance economies.** Egyptian, Yemeni, Palestinian, South Asian, and later Syrian and Sudanese labor flows into the Gulf are the fiscal plumbing of half the region. Remittances to Egypt alone ($30B+/year by the 2020s) exceed Suez revenue. No mechanic represents this.
- **Non-state finance.** Hawala networks, Gulf charitable finance, post-2017 crypto for sanctioned actors (Iran, Hamas, North Korea-linked laundering through regional exchanges), and the FATF grey-list pressure on Turkey and the UAE. Legitimacy is modeled; illicit liquidity is not.
- **Sectarianism as a resource, not flavor.** The Sunni–Shi'a frame became a mobilizable asset after 2003 (Zarqawi's deliberate strategy), weaponized by both Riyadh and Tehran. It should be a *spendable* currency — you can burn sectarian capital for proxy recruitment but it depletes cross-sectarian legitimacy.
- **Climate and water.** The 2006–10 Syrian drought is a plausible contributor to 2011; Iraqi and Iranian water stress (Karun, Tigris headwaters, Turkish GAP dams) is a live inter-state issue; Gulf desalination dependency is a strategic vulnerability. No track represents environmental stress.
- **Gulf–Asia pivot.** By 2020, 70%+ of Gulf crude goes to Asia, not the West. The Hegemon's security guarantee and the customer base have *decoupled*. This is the central structural fact of the final Era and the game has no dial for it.
- **Information space.** Al Jazeera 1996, satellite TV's role in the Second Intifada, social media in 2011, TikTok/Telegram in the 2020s Gaza war. "Media" as a strategy card is thin; this deserves its own track.

## 7. What regional players would find reductive

- **Israelis** will object to "perpetually besieged" as the core identity — it elides the occupation, the settler movement as a domestic political faction, the Haredi demographic question, and the 2023 judicial-reform rupture. "Security + Tech + Normalization" as a victory track reads as a Netanyahu-era press release and leaves no room for a left-Zionist or post-Zionist path.
- **Iranians** across the political spectrum will reject "revolutionary theocratic power" as a totalization. The Green Movement 2009, Mahsa Amini 2022, the reformist-vs-principalist split, and the IRGC-vs-regular-state rivalry are first-order internal politics, not flavor. Iran is not one actor.
- **Saudis and Emiratis** will be insulted by fusion. Post-2015 MBS/MBZ Saudi is *not* the Faisal/Abdullah Kingdom; Vision 2030, NEOM, the Khashoggi rupture, and the Yemen war are a different regime.
- **Turks** will object to "bridge state" — it's a 1990s framing that Erdoğan-era AKP explicitly rejected. Neo-Ottomanism, the Gülenist purge post-2016, the Kurdish war resumption 2015, and the Syrian-refugee domestic politics are the actual Turkish story.
- **Palestinians** will find it intolerable that they appear only as "The Movement" — i.e., as a non-state actor that the map defines by its violence, with no civil society, no PA governance track, no 1993 Oslo structure, and no representation of the post-2007 Gaza–West Bank split.
- **Iraqis and Syrians** reduced to "The Ba'ath → Successor States" erase the Shi'a political parties (Dawa, SCIRI/ISCI, Sadrists), the muhasasa system, the Assad alawite-security-state specifics, and the HTS/Idlib trajectory.
- **Egyptians, Lebanese, Jordanians, Yemenis** are not in the game as protagonists at all, which is itself the statement.

## Prioritized changes

1. **Split Hegemon** into Atlantic and Eurasian seats from Era 2 onward; make China a structural buyer with veto on oil-destination flows by Era 5.
2. **Add Egypt** as a full faction (Praetorian Republic) with army-economy and remittance mechanics.
3. **Replace the Movement identity-swap** with two or three typed non-state seats: Resistance, Jihad, and (optionally) Brotherhood.
4. **Re-cut Eras** on actual regime shifts: 1979, 1991, 2003, 2011, 2015 — or compress to three.
5. **Split "The Kingdom"** into Riyadh/Abu Dhabi/Doha, either as sub-factions or as a mandatory internal-coalition layer.
6. **Upgrade Kurdish actors** from terrain to a non-state player-lite with multi-sponsor leverageability.
7. **Add structural tracks** for remittances, water/climate stress, and sectarian capital; add a gas/LNG commodity separate from oil; add a US-shale dial to the Hegemon.
8. **De-totalize faction identities**: each faction needs at least two internal factions (reformist/hardliner, army/party, settler/secular) that can shift the victory track mid-game. No state was one thing for 50 years.
