# CRESCENT IMPERIUM (v1 Draft)
### A 4X Strategy Game of the Modern Middle East, 1976–2026

> A Twilight-Imperium-scale asymmetric strategy game where 4–7 players take the roles of
> regional powers, non-state movements, and external great powers competing to impose
> their vision of order across the Middle East over five decades.

---

## 1. Design Goals

1. **Scale & depth of TI4** — 6–8 hour session, asymmetric factions, negotiation-heavy,
   multi-axis victory (military / economic / ideological / diplomatic).
2. **Historically grounded, not historically deterministic.** Players re-enact pressures,
   not scripted events. The Shah might survive; Saddam might win 1991.
3. **Treat the subject matter seriously.** The game is about statecraft, legitimacy, and
   the costs of violence — not a body-count sandbox. Civilian suffering is a *mechanical
   penalty* against your legitimacy, not a flavor decoration.
4. **Every faction is playable as a protagonist.** No faction is coded "evil." Each has a
   coherent worldview, real constraints, and real grievances.

---

## 2. Components Overview

- **Map:** Modular hex map of the region (Maghreb → Central Asia → Horn of Africa).
  ~60 regions grouped into 12 **Theaters** (Levant, Gulf, Maghreb, Anatolia, Iranian
  Plateau, Arabian Peninsula, Mesopotamia, Nile, Horn, Hejaz, Kurdistan, Afghan Frontier).
- **Era Deck:** Five ~decade-long **Eras** (1976–85, 1986–95, 1996–2005, 2006–15, 2016–26)
  each with its own event deck and shifting global context (Cold War → Unipolar → Multipolar).
- **Resource tracks:** Oil, Hard Currency, Manpower, Legitimacy, Ideology, Intelligence.
- **Faction boards**, **Unit minis** (regulars, militia, armor, air, proxy cells, intel
  assets), **Agenda cards**, **Doctrine trees**, **Crisis tokens**.

---

## 3. Factions (asymmetric, 7 total; 4–7 play per game)

Each has a **unique victory condition track**, a **doctrine tree**, and a **crisis it
fears** (trigger conditions that cost it points).

| Faction | Core identity | Strength | Fear |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Islamic Republic** (Iran) | Revolutionary theocratic power; proxies abroad, isolation at home | Proxy network, ideological export, oil | Internal legitimacy collapse, sanctions choke |
| **The Kingdom** (Saudi Arabia / Gulf bloc) | Oil wealth + religious legitimacy + US alignment | Petro-diplomacy, air power, soft power via media/finance | Shi'a unrest, succession crisis, oil price collapse |
| **The State** (Israel) | Small, technologically superior, perpetually besieged | Intel supremacy, tech tree, qualitative military edge | Demographic/occupation pressure on legitimacy |
| **The Republic** (Turkey) | Bridge state, NATO + neo-Ottoman reach | Flexible alignment, army, geography | Kurdish question, EU/NATO drift, coups |
| **The Ba'ath** → **The Successor States** (Iraq / Syria) | Secular authoritarian; fragile after rupture | Mass army, chemical doctrine, pan-Arab claim | Sectarian fracture, external invasion |
| **The Movement** (non-state: rotating identity — PLO → Hezbollah → AQ → ISIS depending on era) | Sub-state asymmetric actor | Asymmetric warfare, diaspora, media | Loss of sanctuary, leadership decapitation |
| **The Hegemon** (USA; swaps to USSR/Russia/China influence cards) | External great power | Global reach, finance, carrier strike | Domestic war-fatigue, overreach, credibility loss |

**Balance note:** asymmetric victory tracks mean Saudi ≠ racing Iran on the same metric.
Saudi wins on Prestige + Oil Share; Iran wins on Proxy Reach + Ideological Conversion;
Israel wins on Security + Tech + Normalization; Turkey wins on Spheres of Influence;
Movement wins on Legitimacy Transfer (turning popular sympathy into state recognition);
Hegemon wins on Order Points (keeping the system inside its preferred equilibrium).

## 4. Turn Structure (per round ≈ one "year")

1. **Strategy Phase** — players draft one **Strategy Card** (Intelligence, Mobilization,
   Diplomacy, Energy, Media, Doctrine, Covert Action, Summit). Gives initiative order
   and a special action. (TI4 homage.)
2. **Action Phase** — round-robin single actions: move, build, proxy-activate, sanction,
   negotiate, launch operation, spend intel, etc.
3. **Crisis Phase** — reveal an Era Event. Events are *pressures*, not scripted outcomes:
   e.g., "Oil Shock — Energy prices +2 for 2 rounds; Gulf exporters gain Hard Currency
   but Importers lose Legitimacy." Some events are **Flashpoints** that must be
   resolved by vote or force.
4. **Agenda Phase** — Summit / UN / Arab League / OPEC meetings where players vote on
   world-state modifiers. Votes weighted by Legitimacy, not just military.
5. **Status Phase** — score hidden + public objectives, refresh, advance Era track.

## 5. Core Mechanical Innovations

### 5a. Legitimacy as the central currency
Every faction has a **Legitimacy track**. Almost every aggressive action costs Legitimacy
unless *laundered* through an Agenda vote, a Proxy, or a Great Power umbrella.
**Legitimacy gates objectives.** You cannot convert military gains into permanent score
without legitimacy to back them. This is the mechanical answer to "why don't I just
conquer everything": conquest without legitimacy ⇒ occupation debt ⇒ negative income.

### 5b. Proxy layer
Each Theater has a **Proxy slot**. Factions place Proxy Cells (hidden tokens) to exert
influence without direct deployment. Proxies can be **exposed** by opponent Intel actions,
triggering a Legitimacy hit for the sponsor. This abstracts IRGC-QF, CIA stations, Mossad
stations, Saudi religious networks, etc., symmetrically.

### 5c. Oil as a shared weather system
Oil price is a global track moved by: Gulf production decisions, conflict in producer
regions, and Era events. Oil affects *every* faction's income asymmetrically (producers
gain on high, importers gain on low). Prevents runaway Gulf dominance and creates
strong incentives for OPEC-style negotiation rounds.

### 5d. The Civilian Cost track
A shared, non-faction **Regional Suffering track** advances when large-scale violence
occurs. When it hits thresholds, it triggers:
- Refugee flows (destabilize neighboring Theaters),
- Radicalization (free units for The Movement),
- Great Power intervention opportunities,
- Global Legitimacy penalties for belligerents visible on the track.

Players are mechanically incentivized to *not* escalate past the threshold — costs
outweigh gains. The track is **visible** and **attributed**: everyone sees who caused it.

### 5e. Doctrine trees (tech tree analogue)
Not "tech" — **doctrine**. Research paths include Nuclear Latency, Drone Warfare,
Information Operations, Sanctions Regime, Counterinsurgency, Soft Power, Normalization
Diplomacy. Each unlocks new action types. Nuclear is deliberately slow, expensive, and
triggers heavy international response when pursued — a deterrent, not a win button.

### 5f. Era transitions reshape the board
At each Era boundary: faction boards flip (The Ba'ath splits into Iraq/Syria post-2003;
The Movement's identity card changes; The Hegemon's global context shifts Cold War →
Unipolar → Multipolar). This prevents first-Era dominance from snowballing.

---

## 6. Victory

**No single-axis win.** Each faction scores on:
- **Public Objectives** (shared, revealed per Era — e.g., "Control 2 Oil Theaters,"
  "Hold a Summit Resolution," "Reduce Rival's Legitimacy below 3").
- **Secret Objectives** (faction-specific, ~2 held, pursued quietly).
- **Faction Victory Track** (unique per faction — see §3).

Game ends at end of Era 5. Highest weighted score wins. **Pyrrhic victory rule:**
if Regional Suffering track is maxed, a *shared penalty* applies to all players
proportional to their contribution — possible for "the region" to lose even if a
player technically has the most points.

---

## 7. Open design questions flagged for playtest

- Is 5 Eras × ~6 rounds/Era × 7 players tractable under 8 hours? Likely not. Probably
  need to compress to 3 Eras or shorten rounds.
- How do we prevent Hegemon from being either kingmaker or irrelevant?
- Movement faction risks feeling "villainous by default" — does the identity-swap
  mechanic actually mitigate this, or make it worse?
- Sensitivity: are there representations we should not mechanize at all?
- Can Israel faction be played without the game implicitly endorsing or condemning
  specific policies? Same question for every faction.
- Nuclear doctrine: does it need a hard cap to prevent game-ending escalation?
